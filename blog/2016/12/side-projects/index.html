Side Projects Posted on 12/16/2016

I've recently realized that the reason I don't have many finished or polished projects isn't due to a lack of motivation, but more so an inability to focus on one of them at a time. One of the fundamental reasons I enjoy programming so much is the creative freedom that comes with it. These days, software development is mostly limited by your imagination, your willingness to learn new things, and your dedication to finishing what you've started.

It's that final part that turns out to be much more difficult than one would assume. Almost every developer knows the "ninety-ninety rule", but nevertheless, I'm willing to bet we all have drastically underestimated how long a project will take to complete at some point or another. As a result, I have a half-dozen different unfinished projects I'm trying to complete simultaneously, and they all compete to hinder my overall productivity.

I'm reminded of this excellent comic by CommitStrip. On the bright side, I'm still actively learning new languages and practicing more efficient ways to accomplish my goals. After all, the more time spent coding, the better (hopefully) one gets at it.

As for what I've been working on, I've been polishing several things in Broken Shadows before I move on to add some new key features to the game. I've began work on a new platformer in C++ that uses an extremely useful application called Tiled to easily generate levels created from tilesets, along with animations, collision polygons, and spawn points. I also have quite a few ideas for mod development with existing games.

If you happen to read this post by the end of 2016, I wish you happy holidays and the best of luck to you in your endeavors!
